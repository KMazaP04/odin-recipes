Mississippi Slugburger

Picture of Mississippi Slugburger

WARNING: I do not include whole list of ingredients and steps. This burger looks kind of gross but had an interesting name which is why I decided to use it for this practice project.

I will continue this project simply to get practice with git and vscode editor. The food I have presented here is simply just for show and I have no intention on creating this dish.

Ingredients

    7 slices potato bread
  • 1 pound ground chuck
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ¼ teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 cup vegetable oil for frying, or as needed

Steps

  1. Step 1 Preheat the oven to 175 degrees F (80 degrees C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Step 2 Cut the crusts off the bread slices. Place slices on the prepared baking sheet.
  3. Step 3 Bake in the center of the preheated oven until dry and toasted, about 45 minutes.
  4. Step 4 Transfer toasted bread to the bowl of a food processor and pulse until you reach coarse crumbs.
  5. Step 5 Place ground chuck, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder into a bowl. Add bread crumbs. Combine with your fingers, breaking apart the meat.
  6. Step 6 Dampen your fingers with water and form mixture into hamburger patties.
  7. Step 7 Heat ¼ to ½ inch vegetable oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Place a patty into the pan and turn heat down to medium. Cook until well browned and crusty, 3 to 4 minutes per side. Repeat with remaining patties, adding more oil if needed.
  8. Step 8 Remove from the pan and serve on hamburger buns with mustard and pickles; sprinkle tops of patties with chili powder and/or cayenne if you like prior to serving.
